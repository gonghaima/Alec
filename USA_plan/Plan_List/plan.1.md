# Roadmap for a 13-Year-Old in Sydney Aiming for US University Study in Computing/Technology

Your son is already in a strong position: he’s **good at math**, **active in tennis**, and still early enough in high school to build a world‑class profile for US university admissions. Below is a structured, practical plan tailored to his strengths and long‑term goals.

---

# 🎯 What US Universities Look For (Especially in Computing/Tech)

## 🧠 1. Strong Academics

- High performance in mathematics
- Solid science foundation
- Strong reading and writing skills (important for essays)

## 💻 2. Evidence of Tech Interest

- Coding experience
- Personal projects
- Competitions (math, coding, robotics)
- A portfolio showing initiative

## 🎾 3. A “Spike” Extracurricular

Tennis can be a major advantage:

- Shows discipline and long‑term commitment
- Potential for athletic recruitment
- Adds uniqueness to his profile

## 🌟 4. Leadership & Initiative

- Starting a club
- Coaching younger students
- Running events or small projects
- Volunteering

---

# 🧭 Age 13–14: Foundation Building

## 🧩 Academics

- Keep him ahead in maths
- Encourage reading and writing
- Consider competitions:
  - Australian Maths Competition (AMC)
  - ICAS
  - Bebras Computational Thinking
  - Junior Maths Olympiad

## 💻 Early Tech Exposure

Suggested beginner-friendly languages:

- Python
- JavaScript
- Scratch (if needed)

Simple project ideas:

- A small game
- A tennis match tracker
- A maths quiz generator
- A personal website

## 🎾 Tennis Development

- School team
- Local tournaments
- Aim for consistent improvement
- Consider coaching younger kids later (leadership)

---

# 🧭 Age 14–16: Build the “Spike” + Portfolio

## 💻 Tech/Computing Growth

Activities to consider:

- Robotics or coding clubs
- Hackathons
- FIRST Robotics
- Cyber competitions
- Bebras (advanced levels)

Portfolio development:

- 2–3 meaningful coding projects
- GitHub profile
- A small blog or YouTube channel explaining concepts

## 🎾 Tennis

- Regular tournaments
- Aim for state-level ranking
- Coaching or volunteering at the club

## 🗂️ Portfolio Tracking

Keep records of:

- Awards
- Certificates
- Project links
- Videos of tennis matches
- Leadership roles
- Volunteering

---

# 🧭 Age 16–18: US Admissions Preparation

## 📝 SAT/ACT

- Begin light prep around 15–16
- Strong math score is a major advantage

## 🧪 Optional Advanced Subjects

(Not required in Australia, but helpful)

- AP Computer Science
- AP Calculus
- University-level online courses (edX, Coursera)

## 🏫 Summer Programs

Highly valued by US universities:

- Coding camps
- Research programs
- Engineering workshops
- Online university courses

## 🧾 Application Components

- Essays
- Recommendation letters
- Portfolio
- Activity list
- Interviews (for some schools)
