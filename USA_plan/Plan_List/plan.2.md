# Path for a Sydney Student (Age 13) to Study Technology in the United States

If your son is **13 in Sydney**, good at **math and tennis**, and you
want him to study **computing or technology in the United States**, this
is a realistic goal with early planning. Many international students
enter U.S. universities through a combination of **academics, sports,
and extracurricular achievements**.

---

# 1. Three Main Paths to U.S. Universities

## 1. Academic Excellence Path

Focus: **Math, coding, and competitions**

Typical universities: - MIT - Stanford - Carnegie Mellon - UC Berkeley

They look for:

- Very strong grades
- Olympiad or math competitions
- Coding projects
- Research or leadership activities

This path is **very competitive** but possible with strong achievements.

---

## 2. Tennis Recruitment Path

Focus: **tennis + solid academics**

College sports are organized by the NCAA.

Divisions:

Division Level Scholarship

---

Division I Elite High
Division II Strong Medium
Division III Good Low or none

Tennis level is measured by **UTR (Universal Tennis Rating)**.

Typical levels:

Level UTR

---

Top NCAA D1 13--15
Good D1 11--13
D2 10--12
D3 9--11

If your son currently has **UTR \~4 at age 12--13**, that is normal.\
To be recruited by many universities he may need roughly **UTR 10--12 by
age 17**.

---

## 3. Balanced Profile (Best Strategy)

Combine:

- Good academics
- Strong tennis
- Technology projects

This provides **more admission opportunities and scholarship chances**.

Good universities with strong computer science and sports:

- Georgia Tech
- University of Michigan
- University of Texas at Austin
- University of Illinois Urbana‑Champaign

---

# 2. What Your Son Should Do From Age 13--15

## Academics

Encourage:

- Math competitions (AMC style or Australian Maths Competition)
- Begin learning programming

Recommended languages:

- Python
- JavaScript

Example projects:

- Small games
- Websites
- AI experiments
- Robotics

---

## Tennis

If tennis is serious, aim for **3--4 training sessions per week**.

Important goals:

- Play tournaments
- Improve match experience
- Increase UTR rating

Example milestone targets:

Age Target UTR

---

14 6--7
15 7--8

---

## Activities U.S. Universities Like

- Coding projects
- Robotics clubs
- Science competitions
- Hackathons
- Tennis tournaments

---

# 3. Age 15--17 (Critical Period)

## Academics

Aim for:

- High math grades
- Programming competitions
- Building real projects

Examples:

- Mobile apps
- AI tools
- Startup ideas
- Open‑source contributions

---

## Tennis Targets

Age Target UTR

---

16 8--9
17 10--11

At this stage **college coaches start noticing players**.

---

# 4. How Tennis Recruitment Works

Around **age 16--17**:

Students prepare a **player profile** to send to college coaches.

Typical information:

- Match videos
- UTR rating
- Tournament results
- Academic scores

Coaches may offer:

- Athletic scholarship
- Admission support

---

# 5. Typical Cost of U.S. Universities

Estimated yearly cost for international students:

Type Cost

---

Top private universities \~\$80,000 USD/year
State universities \~\$45,000--60,000
With sports scholarship \~\$10,000--40,000

Athletic recruitment can significantly reduce cost.

---

# 6. Important Exams Later

Students usually take these in **Year 11 or 12**:

- SAT or ACT
- TOEFL or IELTS (English test)

---

# 7. Common Mistake Parents Make

Focusing **only on school marks**.

U.S. universities evaluate the **entire student profile**, including:

- Projects
- Achievements
- Sports
- Leadership
- Initiative

---

# 8. Advantages Your Son Already Has

Your son already has two strong advantages:

- Good mathematics ability
- Tennis background

This combination can be **very attractive to U.S. universities**,
especially if he develops **technology projects while continuing tennis
competition**.

---

# Summary Strategy

Best approach:

1.  Continue developing **tennis competitively**
2.  Build **coding and technology projects**
3.  Participate in **math competitions**
4.  Start planning **U.S. college applications around age 16--17**

This balanced path gives the **highest chance of admission and
scholarships**.
