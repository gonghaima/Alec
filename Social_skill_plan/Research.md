# Social Skill Development Plan for Alec — Castle Hill, Sydney

## Legal Context (NSW)

There is **no minimum age** for work in NSW, but at 13 the work must:
- Be light and safe (no hazardous environments)
- Not interfere with school hours
- Have parental consent

Permitted industries at 13 include: **newspaper/pamphlet delivery, retail (including trolley collection), and restaurant/café** settings.

---

## Option 1: Paid Part-Time Work

| Role | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Newspaper/pamphlet delivery** | Very doable at 13, early mornings, builds discipline |
| **Supermarket trolley collection** | Hills District has several large centres (Castle Towers, etc.) — Woolworths/Coles hire young teens |
| **Café/restaurant busboy** | Weekend shifts, excellent for social interaction |
| **Lawn mowing / odd jobs** | Self-employed, can start immediately in the neighbourhood |
| **Tutoring younger kids in math** | His strength — could tutor primary school kids locally (can start via word of mouth) |

---

## Option 2: Volunteering (Great for Social Skills)

These are ideal if formal employment is hard to land at 13:

- **The Hills Shire Council** has a volunteering program — community events, bush regeneration, local festivals. Good entry point with structured social interaction.
- **SEEK Volunteer / GoVolunteer** list 1,400–1,500 opportunities within Castle Hill — many are youth-friendly.
  - [volunteer.com.au – Castle Hill](https://www.volunteer.com.au/volunteering/in-castle-hill-nsw-2154)
  - [govolunteer.com.au – Castle Hill](https://govolunteer.com.au/volunteering/in-castle-hill-nsw)
- **NSW Volunteering** lists 1,555 opportunities near Castle Hill: [makeadifference.volunteering.nsw.gov.au](https://makeadifference.volunteering.nsw.gov.au/volunteering/in-castle-hill-nsw-2154)

---

## Option 3: Leverage Tennis (His Strength)

Using something he's already good at is the best angle to open him up socially:

- **Castle Hill Tennis Academy (CHTA)** — a long-running local academy running school holiday camps and group lessons.
  - He could join junior competitive programs (structured team environment)
  - Enquire about being a **junior coaching assistant** — helping with younger kids' sessions
  - Website: [chta.com.au](https://chta.com.au)
- **Hills District Tennis Association (HDTA)** — the umbrella association. Junior competitions force social interaction in a low-pressure way.
  - Website: [hdta.org.au](https://hdta.org.au)

---

## Option 4: Structured Social Clubs

If he's not ready for work, these ease him in:

- **Hills District Athletics Club** — multi-age, team training environment
- **Hills Basketball Association (Hills Hornets)** — competitive but very social
- **Coding/math clubs** through The Hills Shire Council or local libraries — plays to his strength while meeting peers

---

## Recommendation

Given his profile (strong in math, strong in tennis, introverted), a **two-track approach** is suggested:

1. **Short term**: Enrol him in a junior tennis competition at HDTA — familiar environment, instant social structure with peers
2. **Medium term**: Approach CHTA about assisting with holiday camps — gives him a job-like role with a social purpose, without the cold-start of formal employment
